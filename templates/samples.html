SAMPLES

Based on the United Nations' Sustainable Goals

Ensure equitable and quality education

Goal: Your goal is to work collaboratively on advancing Nerbo & Ditro project. It couples humanities with science, delivering as a foundation International Baccalaureate environment - Individuals and Societies.

Role: You are an educator who admires hurdles and confronting yourself to ensure equitable and quality education for your local and global environment. (for educators mainly)

Audience: Your audience consists of your students, their parents, and management. It may get bigger and bigger.

Situation: You want to introduce the idea of STEAM education in the Individuals and Societies subject group. You may use the platform, thanks to which one will build scenarios for the students.

Product: Your final product comprises a scenario applying the G.R.A.S.P.S. framework, robots for your students, prepared documentation, and a detailed lesson plan.

Standards: Please, remember to read the Arduino, Raspberry Pi, Sphero R.V.R., and/or JavaScript documentation. You may consider adopting the right Robot Type from Sphero R.V.R., Sphero Bolt, Sphero SPRK+, Sphero 2.0, up to Sphero Mini or Sphero Indi, and even Botzees or Botland's designs. There will be pressure to differentiate enterprises for students. You may think of implementing the following strategies: draw programming, block programming, and/or text programming adopted from Sphero Edu.

Life on land

Goal: Your goal is to formulate and use functions (if possible) to exhibit the problem of desertification, land degradation, and biodiversity loss by combing the global history with STEAM.

Role: You are an expert in natural history, facing a dilemma to make the subject matter recognizable by a worldwide community. (for all students)

Audience: Your audience consists of everybody interested in life on land and issues associated with it.

Situation: You have been reflecting on multiple ways how people have been sustainably affected forests, battled desertification, paused and shifted land degradation, and stopped biodiversity loss throughout history.

Product: You want to create an animated timeline for historical events.

Standards: Please, remember to read the Arduino, Raspberry Pi, Sphero R.V.R., and/or JavaScript documentation. You may consider adopting the right Robot Type from Sphero R.V.R., Sphero Bolt, Sphero SPRK+, Sphero 2.0, up to Sphero Mini or Sphero Indi, and even Botzees or Botland's designs. You may think of operating on the following strategies: draw programming, block programming, and/or text programming adopted from Sphero Edu.

Inspiration: Sphero Edu Scenario: Storytelling 5: Titanic Functions

Audience: Your audience is the local neighborhood. (for MYP Years 4&5, and DP students)

Implement and revitalize the Global Partnership for Sustainable Development

Goal: Your main objective is to build your first responsive robot by applying loops and conditions (if possible).

Role: You are an enthusiastic and productive student. You would like to become an entrepreneur in the most flourishing prospect these days, namely SpaceX. (for all students)

Audience: Your audience will consist of a group of people working with you at first. Then the idea is to make it a little bit more diverse and international.

Situation: To make your dream come true, please explore an opening to constitute a global partnership with students in other I.B. schools while arranging your final product. Remember that we are living in the age of technology and globalization. Please, use it wisely!

Product: A warm-up session for you is to design a moveable vehicle that will report findings with humans by using colors: green means "I've got it!"; yellow - "I'm looking for it!" and red - "Ups, this isn't what I'm looking for!".
